The operating thesis

Make AP's best client work compound.

The goal is not more AI features. It is a product system that turns recurring expert work into governed, adopted capability - without flattening the judgment clients hire AP to provide.

A hypothesis for discovery: the role exists to convert APVision's proprietary data and emerging AI capability into reusable products that improve client outcomes, decision speed, service leverage, and differentiation.

What becomes easier to decide

A product portfolio needs an operating scorecard.

No unsupported maturity scores. Establish baselines, units, owners, and evidence before targets. Measure both the client outcome and the operating mechanism that created it.

Client outcome

Revenue contribution, conversion, incremental value, opportunity capture, risk avoided.

Work economics

Cycle time, manual effort avoided, rework, support burden, reuse across accounts.

Adoption

Active use by intended teams, workflow penetration, accepted recommendations, repeat use.

Trust & quality

Review outcomes, precision, false alarms, policy exceptions, data-control adherence.

Learning speed

Time from hypothesis to evidence, failed assumptions retired, reusable components harvested.

Ownership

Named product, workflow, data, benefit, and human-decision owners after launch.

The hard objection

Name the gap. Test the transfer.

A credible campaign should address the hiring manager's strongest reasonable concern without turning the entire candidacy into an apology.

“You have not held the formal title of AI Product Director in a partnership marketing agency.”

Correct. I would not pretend domain adjacency is domain equivalence.

The transfer case is that I have repeatedly worked at the boundary this role must manage: emerging technology, proprietary operating context, cross-functional users, human authority, quality, adoption, and measurable outcomes. My first move would be to learn AP's client and account workflows deeply with the people who own them - then use product discipline to make the strongest patterns reusable without stripping away expert judgment.

The interview test: put a current workflow and a hard product decision on the table. I will show how I frame the outcome, surface the risks, define ownership, and design the evidence needed to scale or stop.

Executive discovery questions

Questions I would bring into the room.

These are designed to expose the actual product mandate, the organizational contract, and where the role can create leverage quickly.

Which current AI-enabled workflows are genuinely embedded in weekly client work, and which remain impressive demonstrations?

Where does APVision create the most differentiation today: proprietary data, workflow integration, decision support, speed, or the combination?

What must be standardized across clients, and where must the product preserve regional, brand, or program-specific judgment?

Who owns benefit realization after a capability launches - product, analytics, account leadership, or a shared operating contract?

What human decisions must remain explicit in client-facing recommendations, and where is bounded autonomy acceptable?

How will leadership balance client value, service margin, adoption, reuse, and differentiation when prioritizing the portfolio?
