## Typography evidence and decision
The public site presents a clean geometric/humanist sans-serif character, uppercase micro-headings, strong weight contrast, generous headline scale, and compact navigational labels. No licensed official font files are copied. The campaign uses broadly available Avenir Next/Century Gothic/Trebuchet fallbacks for display and Segoe UI/Arial for body text, reproducing the structural character without claiming an official typeface.

## Photography / illustration character
The campaign intentionally does not use client or employee photography because the role thesis is better explained through a product-decision mechanism. The original Service-to-Product Fold borrows only the company's angular geometry and color recognition layer.

## Geometry and interface motifs
- Angular, open interlocking form inspired by the official mark without recreating it
- Bold color planes against navy/white fields
- Crisp rules, restrained rounded corners, high information clarity
- Teal/blue as the employer-recognition layer; gold as a subordinate candidate-original decision accent

## Motion character
Calm, deliberate folding and locking states explain whether a workflow has sufficient outcome, trust, reuse, and proof to earn a stronger product posture. Motion is stateful and role-derived, not a generic scroll reveal. The reduced-motion mode removes animation while preserving all states and content.

## Recommended use by artifact
- Website: fullest brand expression, official logo above fold, fold metaphor, official palette
- Resume and cover letter: one shared clean header, restrained company line and logo, navy/teal rules
- Brief, plan, objection analysis, canvas: same header, page furniture, typography, and evidence colors

## Independent-candidate distinction
All surfaces identify the work as independent candidate analysis. No fake AP navigation, letterhead, endorsement, employee portal, proprietary interface, or undisclosed internal claim is presented.
